About Me

Xavier Bourque

Visual Effects Specialist

When I joined the VFX industry in 1998, I was immediately fascinated by the unique combination of talents that is required to complete successful shots. The combination of interesting people, art and science makes VFX a very special kind of work. Today, I am still seeking the thrill of doing what was deemed impossible only a short time ago. This explains my continued commitment to VFX, but also my passion about photography and cinematography. These so-called 'traditional' fields are now being transformed to their core by new technology, creating a brave new world just begging to be explored.

If your team is composed of interesting people and you need to solve cutting edge challenges involving a healthy mix of art and technique, I'm sure my experience can help.

Vicker's Screen

Stereoscopic integration of footage into “Vicker’s Screen”. Work includes generating snow using Nuke’s particle system, stereo conversion of snowscape matte painting, digital glitching of “Violin Girl” footage and preparing stereo conversion assets of “Lawrence of Arabia” footage for external vendor.

  • Compositor: Xavier Bourque
  • Year: 2012
  • Show: Prometheus
  • Director: Ridley Scott
  • Facility: Fuel VFX, Sydney, Australia

The Orrery

Stereoscopic set extensions. Work includes matching on-set dynamic lighting, warping CG elements to match in-camera depth and in one case a full stereo re-conversion (middle shot, below.).

  • Compositors: Xavier Bourque & Denis Scolan
  • Year: 2012
  • Show: Prometheus
  • Director: Ridley Scott
  • Facility: Fuel VFX, Sydney, Australia

Maskmaker

Integration of CG “Mask Maker”. Work was performed at IMAX resolution (4k, full aperture) and includes removing practical prop from the plates.

  • Director: Brad Bird
  • Facility: Fuel VFX, Sydney, Australia
